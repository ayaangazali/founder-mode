# Launch Playbook

Distribution plan derived from the verified research (RESEARCH-REPORT.md §6–§7). The one-line version: **Hacker News ignites, LinkedIn amplifies, the badge does the compounding.**

## Pre-flight checklist
- [ ] Game live on a public URL, tested on desktop + phone
- [ ] OG preview image renders (test: [linkedin.com/post-inspector](https://www.linkedin.com/post-inspector/))
- [ ] v0.2 items done — especially the acceptance-letter rename (ROADMAP §2)
- [ ] You've beaten the game yourself and saved YOUR badge PNG (you need it for the posts)
- [ ] Analytics events firing (if using)

## Launch day (pick a Tue/Wed/Thu; be free-ish all morning US Pacific time)

### ~7:30–8:30am PT — Show HN
The verified ignition channel: 2048 went to #1 on HN via a third-party post and cascaded everywhere from there.
- Title, in the proven shape (plain, first-person, no marketing adjectives):
  > **Show HN: I made a Mario-style browser game about surviving SF startup culture**
- URL: the game itself, not a blog post.
- Immediately add one first comment with context: built it for fun in a weekend-ish, one HTML file, no frameworks, what you'd add next. HN loves scope discipline and technical honesty; it converts to upvotes.
- **Stay in the thread all morning answering comments.** Engagement in hour one heavily affects trajectory.
- Do not submit from a brand-new account, don't ask friends to upvote (HN detects voting rings).

### Same morning — X/Twitter
- Post a 20–40s screen-capture GIF/video of gameplay (the SYNERGY.AI fight or a badge reveal is the money shot), link in the same post or first reply.
- Quote-tweet-able framing: "I got tired of writing AI B2B SaaS decks, so I made a game where you fight one."

### Same day, ~11am–1pm PT — LinkedIn (your stated main target)
Write it as a **founder story with an image**, not a launch announcement:
- Attach your own badge PNG as a native image (not a link card).
- **Game URL goes in the first comment** and inside the badge image itself — LinkedIn is widely believed to downrank external links in post bodies; the image+comment pattern is the standard workaround. (Honest note: LinkedIn-specific dynamics were the one area the research couldn't verify with a case study — this is the practitioner-consensus play, and the badge/copy-post flow is built for exactly it.)
- Post skeleton:
  > I spent [X] building an AI B2B SaaS company. Last weekend I built a game where you fight one instead.
  >
  > FOUNDER MODE: a tiny Mario-style browser game about surviving SF. You stomp churn, dodge term sheets from a VC in a vest, and the final boss is an AI SaaS company called SYNERGY.AI. When you lose, the game tells you it was a market timing issue.
  >
  > It's free, it's dumb, it takes 4 minutes. My badge below — beat my time and I'll repost yours. Link in comments.
- That last line ("beat my time and I'll repost yours") is the compounding loop: every reply with a badge is another impression of the game inside other people's networks. Actually repost the good ones.

### Day 2–3 — Product Hunt (optional)
Lower priority than HN for a free joke game, but cheap: launch with the tagline "The startup simulator where the final boss is an AI B2B SaaS company."

## The compounding mechanic (why the badge matters more than any post)
Wordle sat at 90 players for two months; the share-grid turned every *player* into a distributor (1.2M grid tweets in 13 days). Your equivalents:
- Win badge → flex post
- **Loss badge → self-deprecating founder-humor post (likely your best performer on LinkedIn)**
- Leaderboard (v0.3) → "beat my time" replies

Optimize relentlessly for "player reaches a badge screen and the copy button works on their phone."

## Timing tailwind
Universal Paperclips' creator credited "the meme weather" — active AI discourse — for its spread. AI-fatigue jokes are at max cultural liquidity right now. Don't sit on the launch for months polishing.

## What NOT to do
- Don't buy ads/promotion — every case study spread organically; paid smells wrong for a joke project.
- Don't gate anything behind email/signup.
- Don't launch on a Friday or a holiday week.
- Don't argue with anyone who says it's stupid. "That's the point" + keep it moving.
